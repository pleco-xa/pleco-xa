================================================================================
                           KEY INSIGHTS
================================================================================

1. MASSIVE SCOPE
   Librosa is a comprehensive library with 350+ functions. Full parity 
   would require ~320 hours of development. Focus on core 50-60 functions
   for 95% of use cases.

2. MATHEMATICAL CONSISTENCY
   All functions follow predictable patterns:
   - Accept flexible input (raw audio or pre-computed features)
   - Support multiple units (time, frames, samples)
   - Return consistently shaped arrays
   - Use NumPy/SciPy throughout

3. FEATURE EXTRACTION IMPORTANCE
   Feature extraction (60+ functions) is the largest component.
   Prioritize spectral features, chroma, and MFCC for most audio ML tasks.

4. PITCH TRACKING IS COMPLEX
   Pitch tracking (YIN, pYIN, PipTrack) are among the most complex
   algorithms. Consider whether you need production-grade accuracy or
   if basic autocorrelation suffices.

5. UNIT CONVERSIONS ARE PERVASIVE
   60+ functions handle conversions between:
   - Time domains (frames, samples, seconds)
   - Frequency domains (Hz, MIDI, notes, cents, semitones)
   - Frequency scales (linear, log, mel, octaves, Svara, FJS)
   These are worth implementing early for flexibility.
