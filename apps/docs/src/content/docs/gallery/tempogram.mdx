A tempogram is a local autocorrelation of the onset envelope: one column per
frame, brightness by candidate tempo. This demo feeds it a click train that
jumps from 100 BPM to 140 BPM partway through, and the ridge in the heatmap
tracks the change — within one lag bin of 100 for the first stretch and of 140
for the second. Running `estimate_tempo` on each half lands in the matching
bracket.

<DemoEmbed src="xa-tempogram.html" title="Tempogram of a tempo jump" height={720} />

The onset envelope comes from `onset_strength`; the heatmap is `tempogram`
(with `fourier_tempogram` shown alongside), and the per-half readout is
`estimate_tempo`. Lag/BPM bookkeeping uses the `convert` namespace.
