`pyin` estimates the fundamental frequency of monophonic audio and, unlike a
bare autocorrelation peak-picker, runs a genuine HMM/Viterbi decode to smooth
the contour and label voiced versus unvoiced frames. This demo tracks a signal
that steps from 220 Hz to 330 Hz: the plateaus land within a semitone, the HMM
cleanly resolves the jump, and the silent tail decodes as unvoiced instead of
guessing a pitch.

<DemoEmbed src="pyin.html" title="pYIN probabilistic pitch tracking" height={680} />

The call is `pyin(y, fmin, fmax, sr)`, returning the f0 contour alongside a
per-frame voiced probability in `[0, 1]`. The page resynthesizes the tracked
contour so you can hear the estimate against the source.
